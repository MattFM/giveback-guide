## Using Search and Filters

### Basic Search

Start by entering keywords in the search bar on the [projects page](/projects/). 
You can search for:

- **Countries or regions** — "Thailand", "Costa Rica", "East Africa"
- **Types of work** — "teaching", "conservation", "construction"
- **Organisations** — Specific charity or NGO names
- **Skills** — "medical", "veterinary", "engineering"

### Filter Options

Use the filter buttons to narrow results by:

- **Country** — Select one or multiple countries
- **Impact Areas** — Education, animal welfare, conservation, etc.
- **Duration** — Minimum and maximum time commitments
- **Cost** — Free opportunities, low cost, or higher investment
- **Skills Required** — Beginner-friendly or specialist roles
- **Accommodation** — Shared housing, homestay, independent, etc.

### Sorting Results

Once you have filtered results, sort them by:

- **Relevance** — Best match to your search terms
- **Recently added** — Newest listings first
- **Alphabetical** — By organisation or project name

## Reading Project Listings

### What to Look For

Each project listing includes several key sections. Here's how to read them:

#### Project Header

- **Project name** — The specific opportunity title
- **Organisation** — Who runs the project; click to learn more about them
- **Location** — City/region and country; click location tags to find similar projects
- **Impact areas** — Categories like "Education" or "Animal Welfare"
- **Verification badge** — Indicates we've thoroughly vetted this project

#### Quick Facts

- **Duration** — Minimum time commitment required
- **Cost** — Whether it's free or has associated fees
- **Accommodation** — Living arrangements provided
- **Meals** — Whether food is included
- **Airport pickup** — Whether transport is provided

#### Project Description

Read carefully to understand:

- What the project does and why
- What your role would involve day-to-day
- What skills or experience are needed
- Who benefits from the work
- How success is measured

#### Organisation Information

Check the organisation's profile to learn:

- Their mission and history
- Other projects they run
- Financial transparency
- Reviews from previous volunteers

## Understanding Verification

### What the Badge Means

Projects with the Giveback Guide verification badge have passed our rigorous assessment:

- ✓ Thoroughly researched background and practices
- ✓ Evidence of genuine, sustainable impact
- ✓ Local community involvement and leadership
- ✓ Transparent operations and finances
- ✓ High ethical standards (especially for animal welfare)
- ✓ Good volunteer support and preparation

[Read about our full verification process](/docs/policies/verification/).

### Unverified Projects

You may find some listings without verification badges. These are either:

- **Newly added** — Currently being reviewed
- **Under review** — Being re-verified
- **User-submitted** — Suggested by community members, pending review

For unverified projects, do additional research. Look for independent reviews, contact 
previous volunteers directly, and ask tough questions before committing.

## Assessing Fit

### Be Honest About Your Skills

Good volunteering isn't about good intentions alone—it's about genuine contribution. 
Ask yourself:

- Do I have the specific skills this project needs?
- Am I experienced enough for the responsibilities described?
- Is my language proficiency adequate?
- Do I have relevant certifications (medical, teaching, etc.)?

### Consider Your Constraints

- **Time** — Can I commit for the minimum duration required?
- **Budget** — Do I understand all costs (program fees, flights, visas, insurance)?
- **Health** — Am I physically and mentally prepared for this environment?
- **Comfort** — Am I okay with the accommodation and living conditions?

### Red Flags to Avoid

Be cautious of projects that:

- Promise "life-changing experiences" without specifics about the work
- Require little to no skills or preparation
- Have minimal local involvement or leadership
- Can't provide evidence of their impact
- Focus more on the volunteer's experience than the community's benefit
- Have poor or defensive responses to questions

## Saving and Comparing

### Using Lists

Found several interesting projects? Create lists to organise them:

- **"Thailand 2025"** — Shortlist for specific trip
- **"Teaching Opportunities"** — By type of work
- **"Compare Later"** — Options to research further

To add a project to a list, click the "Save" button on any project page and select or 
create a list.

### Comparing Options

When comparing projects side by side, consider creating a simple spreadsheet with:

- Project name and organisation
- Location and dates available
- Total cost (fees + estimated additional expenses)
- Skills match (1-10)
- Impact potential (based on research)
- Support provided (training, accommodation, etc.)
- Reviews score

## Next Steps

Once you've found a promising project:

1. **Research thoroughly** — Read every section of the listing, check the organisation's website
2. **Contact the organisation** — Ask specific questions about the role, expectations, and support
3. **Connect with past volunteers** — Use our review system or ask the organisation for references
4. **Prepare realistically** — Understand the challenges, not just the highlights
5. **Apply early** — Good projects fill up; apply well in advance
