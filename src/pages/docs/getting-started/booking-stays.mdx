## What Makes a Stay "Sustainable"?

On Giveback Guide, sustainable stays meet rigorous criteria:

- **Community benefit** — Locally owned or significant community involvement
- **Cultural preservation** — Respects and celebrates local heritage
- **Environmental responsibility** — Genuine practices, not just marketing
- **Economic impact** — Fair wages, local employment, fair trade practices
- **Transparency** — Honest about sustainability claims

## Understanding Eco-Credentials

### Green Certifications

Many stays have third-party certifications. Common ones include:

- **Green Globe** — Comprehensive sustainability standard
- **Green Key** — Environmental responsibility in hospitality
- **EarthCheck** — Scientific benchmarking for sustainability
- **Rainforest Alliance** — Environmental and social standards
- **Fair Trade Tourism** — Fair wages and community benefit
- **LEED** — Green building certification

We verify certifications rather than simply accepting logos. A stay with certification 
has met that organisation's standards—but we still conduct our own assessment.

### Beyond Certifications

Many excellent sustainable stays lack formal certification due to cost or bureaucracy. 
We assess these based on actual practices:

- **Energy** — Renewable sources, efficiency measures
- **Water** — Conservation, rainwater harvesting, greywater systems
- **Waste** — Reduction, recycling, composting, elimination of single-use plastics
- **Food** — Local sourcing, organic options, minimal food miles
- **Biodiversity** — Habitat protection, native plants, wildlife corridors
- **Community** — Local employment, fair wages, community projects

## Booking Options

### How Booking Works

Giveback Guide connects you with stays; booking happens through various channels:

- **Direct with property** — Email or phone the stay directly
- **Via their website** — Book through the stay's own booking system
- **Through platforms** — Booking.com, Airbnb, etc. (we show these options)
- **Via responsible tour operators** — Some stays book through ethical travel companies

We never handle payments ourselves. You book directly with the stay or through their 
chosen platforms. This ensures:

- More money goes directly to the stay (no middleman fees)
- You have direct communication with your hosts
- You're covered by the booking platform's policies
- The stay controls their availability and pricing

### Platform Booking

When multiple booking platforms are available, you'll see options like:

- **Booking.com** — Often best for flexible cancellation
- **Airbnb** — Good for longer stays and home-like experiences
- **Expedia** — Sometimes offers package deals with flights
- **Direct** — Often best price, supports stay most directly

We may earn a small commission from platform bookings at no extra cost to you. This 
supports our work while keeping listings free for sustainable stays.

## What to Expect

### Sustainable vs. Conventional

Sustainable stays sometimes differ from conventional accommodation:

- **Smaller scale** — Often family-run or community-owned
- **Simpler amenities** — May lack luxury features, prioritise sustainability
- **Cultural immersion** — More interaction with local community
- **Local food** — Meals feature local ingredients, traditional cuisine
- **Conservation focus** — Wildlife viewing, nature activities emphasised

### Questions to Ask

Before booking, consider asking:

- How is the stay involved in the local community?
- What specific environmental practices do you have?
- Where does the food come from?
- How do you treat wastewater and waste?
- Do you employ locally and pay fair wages?
- How do you support conservation or cultural heritage?

## After Booking

### Preparing for Your Stay

- Read any provided information about local customs and etiquette
- Pack sustainably — reusable water bottles, bags, etc.
- Learn basic phrases in the local language
- Research the local area and community context
- Set realistic expectations (sustainable ≠ luxury)

### During Your Stay

- Follow the stay's sustainability guidelines
- Respect local customs and traditions
- Conserve resources (water, energy) as requested
- Engage with the community respectfully
- Ask questions—hosts usually love sharing their sustainability story

### Leaving a Review

After your stay, please leave a review on Giveback Guide. Your feedback helps:

- Future travellers make informed decisions
- Us monitor that stays maintain their standards
- The stay improve their sustainability practices
- Build a community of conscious travellers

## Troubleshooting

### Common Issues

**Can't find availability?**

- Popular sustainable stays fill up—book well in advance
- Consider shoulder season for better availability
- Contact the stay directly; sometimes they have space not shown online

**Prices seem high?**

- Sustainable practices often cost more (fair wages, organic food, renewable energy)
- Compare what's included (meals, activities, airport transfer)
- Remember: higher prices often mean more money stays in the community

**Concerned about a stay's claims?**

- Contact us—we can provide more details about our verification
- Ask the stay direct questions about specific practices
- Check our reviews from other travellers
