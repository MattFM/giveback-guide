<p class="lead">
Every listing on Giveback Guide undergoes a thorough verification process before appearing 
on our platform. This page explains exactly how we evaluate organisations and what criteria 
we use to determine if they meet our standards.
</p>

## Why Verification Matters

The travel and volunteering industry is plagued by greenwashing, exploitation, and well-meaning 
projects that cause more harm than good. Our verification process exists to protect:

- **Travellers** — From wasting time, money, and effort on ineffective experiences
- **Communities** — From volunteer projects that create dependency or disruption
- **Animals** — From facilities that prioritise tourism over welfare
- **Environments** — From conservation projects that damage ecosystems

We don't list everything. We list things that genuinely make a difference.

## Our Verification Process

### Phase 1: Initial Application Review

Organisations submit detailed information including:

- Legal registration and governance structure
- Mission statement and impact goals
- Financial transparency (funding sources, budget breakdown)
- Staff qualifications and training
- Volunteer role descriptions and expectations
- Accommodation and living conditions for volunteers
- Evidence of past impact (reports, testimonials, data)
- References from previous volunteers and partners

We review every submission to identify red flags and assess whether the organisation's 
goals align with our mission. Many applications are rejected at this stage if they:

- Involve activities we prohibit (orphanage tourism, wildlife exploitation, etc.)
- Show no evidence of community/local leadership
- Have unclear or unrealistic goals
- Demonstrate financial instability
- Lack transparency about funding and operations

### Phase 2: Documentation Review

For organisations passing initial review, we request:

- **Legal documents** — Registration certificates, tax status, permits
- **Financial records** — Budgets, annual reports, funding sources
- **Program materials** — Curricula, protocols, training materials
- **Impact evidence** — Data, reports, third-party evaluations
- **Media and photos** — Unedited images of facilities and work

We verify these documents where possible and look for consistency with the organisation's claims.

### Phase 3: Remote Assessment

We conduct video calls with key staff, review live footage of facilities, and connect with 
references including:

- Previous volunteers (we contact them directly, not just use provided references)
- Local partners and community members
- Other organisations in the same field
- Experts in the organisation's focus area

### Phase 4: Site Visit (When Possible)

For organisations in locations our team can access, we conduct unannounced site visits. 
During these visits we:

- Observe operations without preparation
- Speak with volunteers and staff informally
- Inspect facilities and living conditions
- Verify that practices match documentation
- Assess the genuine impact being made

When we cannot visit directly, we work with trusted local contacts or organisations 
to conduct assessments on our behalf.

### Phase 5: Ongoing Monitoring

Verification doesn't end when an organisation is listed. We continuously monitor:

- Volunteer feedback (we actively solicit reviews)
- Social media and online presence for red flags
- News and reports about the organisation
- Changes in leadership or practices
- Renewal of required licenses and permits

Organisations must undergo re-verification every [time period] or immediately if we 
receive concerning reports.

## Evaluation Criteria

### Impact and Effectiveness

- Does the work produce measurable, positive outcomes?
- Are the methods evidence-based and effective?
- Is the impact sustainable beyond volunteer visits?
- Does the project address root causes, not just symptoms?

### Community Ownership

- Are local people leading the project?
- Does the community define what help they need?
- Are local voices central to decision-making?
- Does the project build local capacity rather than creating dependency?

### Transparency and Accountability

- Is the organisation open about successes and failures?
- Do they share financial information?
- Can they provide evidence of their impact claims?
- Do they respond constructively to criticism?

### Volunteer Experience

- Are volunteer roles clearly defined and appropriate for skills?
- Is pre-departure preparation and support adequate?
- Are living conditions safe and comfortable?
- Is there meaningful work, not just "volunteer tourism"?

### Ethical Standards

- Does the project respect human rights and dignity?
- Are animals treated with appropriate welfare standards?
- Is the environmental impact positive or neutral?
- Does the project avoid exploitation of any kind?

## Scoring and Decisions

Organisations receive scores across each criteria category. To be listed, they must:

- Meet minimum thresholds in all categories
- Score particularly high on ethical standards (non-negotiable)
- Show evidence of continuous improvement
- Demonstrate commitment to our values

Even organisations that meet all criteria may not be listed if we believe similar, 
better options already exist. We prioritise quality over quantity.

## Appeals and Disputes

Organisations that are rejected can appeal if they believe we made an error or if they've 
made significant improvements. We review appeals with fresh eyes and may request additional 
information.

## Your Role in Verification

Verification is a collective effort. If you visit a Giveback Guide-listed organisation:

- **Leave honest reviews** — Your feedback helps us monitor quality
- **Report concerns** — Contact us if you observe problems
- **Share documentation** — Photos and notes help us verify practices
- **Talk to locals** — Community perspectives are invaluable
