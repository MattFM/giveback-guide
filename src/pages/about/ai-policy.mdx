AI is now a prevalent part of everyday life. But at what cost?

Give Back Guide fundamentally exists to facilitate human to human connection through tourism. The race to create AI Large Language Models (LLMs) is, in many ways, at odds with our mission. 

By creating this document, we hope to not only be transparent with our users, but to be honest with ourselves about our AI usage, with an aim to improve our approach to working practices.

This policy document is living, in that it will be constantly updated as we adapt to new information.

## Principles

Generally speaking, we are AI sceptics and remain unconvinced that AI is the answer to society’s many problems. On the contrary, we feel that AI often generates new problems, whilst exacerbating existing ones. 

At the forefront of our principles on AI are the following concerns:

- **LLM's often promote biases** such as sexism, racism, ableism and transphobia.
- **LLM's were trained using stolen content.** We consider the use of copyrighted materials for training as not just unethical, but theft.
- **LLM’s are terrible for the environment.** Their unbridled use of scarce natural resources (including raw materials, energy and water) is a major problem.

With all of this in mind, we strive to be careful and thoughtful with our AI usage.

## Red Lines

There are a number of tasks and functions that we will not use AI for. They are as follows:

- We do not use AI for web searches, unless our search query involves problem solving. In this case, we use [Ecosia](https://www.ecosia.com) which is powered by 100% renewable energy.
- We do not use AI to write newsletters, articles, social media posts or page content.
- We do not process sensitive/personal data using AI.
- We do not use ChatGPT or Perplexity.
- We do not use AI to generate any types of visual media, be it vectors, photos (including photorealistic imagery) or videos. Real photos/videos and human-made vectors are so much better in so many ways.

## How we specifically use AI

In this section, we will explore in detail about how we currently use AI. As previously mentioned, all of this usage is under constant review.

### Written content

We may occasionally use AI for research purposes or to plan an article and/or newsletter, but this is increasingly rare as we believe we can acheieve better results ourselves.

We use AI to rewrite descriptions on project and stay pages, based on existing content from third-party websites and/or social media presences. Our AI agent is instructed to be factual, to rewrite content in the third person and to highlight the tourist accessibility of projects and stays.

### Platform development

We use AI for coding. 

All ideas for the development of the platform initially come from human team members and community members. We then use AI to explore the viability and precise implementation of a new feature. We then use AI to create the code.

Our use of AI helps us to produce accurate and error-free code and helps us to spend more time on content curation and marketing.

Wherever possible, we will write code manually.

### Database management

We use AI agents to run an initial check on projects and stays in our database to see whether they are a good fit for publication. This is strictly a triage process that labels potential listings as either ‘suitable’ or ‘not suitable’. 

Each and every project and stay is still thoroughly manually checked by a human.

If the triage process marks a project or stay as ‘suitable, we use another AI agent to complete data properties in our database. For example, our agent will visit the website of a project or stay and complete the ‘country’ field, the ‘slug’ field and the ‘Impact Areas’ field, among others. 

This eliminates a lot of manual processing and helps to expedite the manual checking process.

## What we’re doing to improve our processes

We are always wary of our AI usage and are constantly seeking to either eliminate aspects or to change how we use it to better meet our sustainability and ethical concerns.

Here’s what we’re currently doing:

- **Testing locally run LLMs.** We are testing out the viability of running LLMs on our own local equipment, rather than relying on LLMs running on remote server farms. Results are increasingly impressive, but there’s still work to be done before we’re able to fully switch.
- **Ethical LLMs.** We would be far more comfortable using LLMs if we knew HOW an LLM was built. We are always keeping ourselves up to date with how an LLMs was built and always seeking genuinely ethical LLMs. Thankfully, as concern grows among general populations about AI, these concerns are increasingly being address and progress is now being made.
