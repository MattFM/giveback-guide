Regenerative travel starts with how we perceive and respect the lands we visit. To travel mindfully is to acknowledge that every destination has a story, and a name, that often predates the maps many of us commonly use.

Today, we are really pleased to introduce a new feature to our platform: Country Aliases and Name Acknowledgments.

## What is this new feature?

We have integrated a new system that recognises indigenous names and endonyms (the names used by people who live there) alongside standard international country names.

When you browse projects or stays on Giveback Guide, you will notice two key changes:

* Direct acknowledgments: On Project and Stay pages, you’ll see the indigenous and/or endonym names listed in italics (e.g., Aotearoa alongside New Zealand).
* Smarter, more inclusive search: You can now find regenerative experiences by searching for either their canonical country name or their alias. For example, whether you search for “Wales” or “Cymru,” you’ll see the same search results.

For now, we’ve introduced this new feature only for a handful of different countries. We’ll be incrementally adding more as time progresses.

## Why does this matter?

Language is a powerful tool for restoration. Many traditional names were erased or replaced during eras of colonisation. By bringing these names back into the conversation, we aim to:

* Show Respect: Acknowledge the sovereignty and heritage of the communities we feature.
* Educate: Help our community of travellers learn the true history of the places they love.
* De-centre the Traveller: Remind ourselves that we are guests on these lands.

Giveback Guide is an English language platform, so our URLs and drop-down menus will continue to use canonical country names. From today though, indigenous names and endonyms are now woven into the fabric of our database.

This is a small step in a long journey toward more conscious travel, and we are delighted to have you walking (and searching) alongside us.
